## FAQ


### What is a Chain Mortiser?

A chain mortiser is a woodworking tool that is used to create mortise joints in wooden frames or furniture. It is designed to make the process of cutting out the recessed area or slot for the tenon (a projecting piece from one piece of wood) more precise and less time-consuming. This tool is essential for people who want to create strong joinery in their wooden structures. 

The chain mortiser is equipped with a cutting chain that can be adjusted in length to accommodate different mortise sizes. It operates by guiding the cutting chain along the workpiece, creating the desired mortise with a clean, accurate edge. This tool has become a popular alternative to traditional mortiser tools, as it is generally faster, easier to use, and more versatile. 


### How does a Chain Mortiser work?

The chain mortiser works by utilizing a cutting chain that is attached to a motor-powered spindle. The cutting chain consists of a series of interlocking teeth that are designed to cut through wood. The user adjusts the length of the chain to match the desired mortise depth, and then places the workpiece on the mortiser table. 

Once the workpiece is securely positioned, the user turns on the machine and guides the cutting chain along the desired mortise line. The cutting action of the chain creates a clean, straight cut that forms the mortise joint. The user can then repeat the process on the opposite side of the workpiece to create a perfectly aligned mortise joint. 


### What are the benefits of using a Chain Mortiser over a traditional mortiser?

The primary advantage of using a chain mortiser over a traditional mortiser is the increased speed and efficiency of creating mortise joints. Chain mortisers are usually faster and can handle larger mortises, which makes them ideal for high-volume production work. They are also more versatile, as they can easily cut mortises in various dimensions and angles, which may not be possible with a traditional mortiser. 

Additionally, chain mortisers tend to provide cleaner cuts and smoother surfaces, which can result in stronger and more visually appealing joints. This attribute is especially important for furniture makers who want to showcase their work and ensure that it meets the highest quality standards. Lastly, chain mortisers are generally easier to use, as they have fewer parts and require less maintenance compared to traditional mortisers. 


### What safety precautions should be taken when using a Chain Mortiser?

When using a chain mortiser, it is essential to follow proper safety precautions to prevent accidents and injuries. The user should always read the manual and familiarize themselves with the machine's operation before attempting to use it. Proper protective gear, such as safety glasses and gloves, should be worn at all times while operating the machine. 

The workpiece should be securely clamped in place to prevent it from shifting during operation. The cutting chain should be checked for wear and damage before each use, and any damaged links should be replaced immediately. Users should also be aware of the machine's noisy and fast-moving components, and avoid wearing loose clothing or jewelry that could get caught in the machine. 


### What types of materials can be used with a Chain Mortiser?

Chain mortisers are designed to cut mortises in various types of wood, including hardwoods, softwoods, and laminated wood. They can also be used to create mortise joints in plywood and other panel materials that are commonly used in furniture and cabinetry construction. It is essential to use a sharp, properly maintained cutting chain for optimal performance and to ensure a clean and precise mortise joint. 

While chain mortisers are best suited for cutting mortises in wood, they can also be used for cutting mortises in synthetic materials such as MDF and particleboard. However, the user should be aware that cutting mortises in non-wood materials may require different cutting speeds and techniques, and that the cutting chain may need to be replaced more frequently due to increased wear and tear. 


### What is the maintenance schedule for a Chain Mortiser?

To ensure that the chain mortiser remains in good working condition and continues to provide accurate and efficient mortise joints, it is essential to maintain the machine according to the manufacturer's guidelines. The cutting chain, in particular, requires regular maintenance to avoid wear and tear, and to ensure that the cutting teeth remain sharp. 

The chain should be checked for wear and damage before each use, and any damaged links should be replaced immediately. Periodic lubrication of the cutting chain and spindle is also crucial to maintain smooth operation and prevent rust or corrosion. Other components, such as bearings and gears, should be checked and lubricated as needed according to the machine's maintenance schedule. Regular cleanings and inspections of the machine can help detect any signs of wear or damage early on, minimizing the risk of unexpected breakdowns.
