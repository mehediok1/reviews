## FAQ


### What is Whole Cumin?

Whole Cumin is a spice derived from the cumin plant (Cuminum cyminum). It refers to the mature, dried seeds of the plant, which have a brownish-yellow color and a slightly sweet, nutty flavor. Cumin is widely used in cooking and is a staple in many cuisines around the world, including Indian, Middle Eastern, and Mexican. 

The small yet powerful cumin seed adds depth and warmth to various dishes, making it a popular choice for flavoring meat, fish, vegetables, and even pickles. It also has numerous health benefits, as it is a rich source of antioxidants, vitamins, and minerals. Whole cumin is often used in its ground form, but using whole cumin adds more flavor and aroma to the dish. 


### What is the difference between Whole Cumin and Ground Cumin?

The difference between Whole Cumin and Ground Cumin lies in the form of the spice - whole cumin is the dried seed of the cumin plant, while ground cumin is the powder made from those seeds. Ground cumin is more convenient to use, as it can be easily added to dishes without the need for grinding or crushing. However, whole cumin has a fresher and more intense flavor, as it retains its essential oils better than ground cumin. 

Whole cumin also releases more of its aromatic oils when heated, providing a more pronounced flavor to the dish. On the other hand, ground cumin has a milder flavor and may lose some of its aromatic compounds when exposed to heat. Because of this, whole cumin is generally preferred in culinary applications where flavor and aroma play a crucial role. 


### How do I store Whole Cumin?

To maintain the freshness and flavor of Whole Cumin, it should be stored properly. Keep the container tightly sealed to prevent any contact with air, moisture, or light, which can cause the spice to lose its potency over time. Store it in a cool, dry place, preferably away from direct sunlight, such as in a pantry, spice rack, or kitchen closet. 

If you plan to use whole cumin frequently, it is advisable to store it in smaller quantities in airtight containers. This helps to prevent contamination or spoilage, as well as making it easier to access the spice when needed. Alternatively, you can also store whole cumin in the freezer to prolong its shelf life. To do this, transfer the required amount of whole cumin into a sealed plastic bag or container, and place it in the freezer. When you need to use it, simply thaw the bag or container for a few minutes and crush the seeds as needed.
