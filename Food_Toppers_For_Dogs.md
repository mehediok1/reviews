## FAQ


### What are food toppers for dogs?

Food toppers for dogs are ingredients or supplements that can be added to a dog's regular food to enhance their nutrition or overall health. They may contain vitamins, minerals, fiber, or protein, and can be used to address specific dietary needs, such as digestive issues or skin problems, or simply to improve the quality of a dog's diet. 

Food toppers come in various forms, including powders, oils, or frozen treats. They can be added to dry or wet dog food, making it more palatable and nutritious for your furry friend. Common ingredients found in food toppers include fruits, vegetables, supplements, and even probiotics for a healthy gut. 


### What are the benefits of using food toppers for dogs?

Food toppers offer several benefits for your dog's health and wellbeing. By adding nutrient-rich ingredients to your dog's food, you can help them maintain optimal health and potentially address any underlying issues. Some of the benefits include: improved digestion, enhanced skin and coat, increased energy levels, and better overall nutrition. 

Additionally, food toppers can make your dog's meals more enjoyable and palatable, which may encourage them to eat more and reduce the chances of picky eating. Lastly, they can be a convenient and easy way to supplement your dog's diet if you're not sure about adding extra ingredients or if you're unable to provide a balanced meal due to time constraints or other factors. 


### Are there any food toppers that I should avoid for my dog?

While food toppers can be beneficial for your dog's health, some ingredients should be avoided. Certain foods, such as chocolate, onions, and garlic, are toxic to dogs and can cause serious health problems if consumed. Additionally, some human foods, like grapes, raisins, and avocado, can be harmful to dogs in large quantities. 

Always consult with your veterinarian before adding a new food topper to your dog's diet, especially if your dog has a history of allergies or health issues. Stick to natural, high-quality ingredients that are specifically formulated for canine consumption, and avoid any artificial preservatives, colors, or additives. 


### How much food topper should I add to my dog's meal?

The amount of food topper you should add to your dog's meal depends on several factors, including your dog's size, age, and overall health. It's essential to follow the manufacturer's recommended dosage and consult with your veterinarian for personalized advice. Generally, a small amount (1/4 cup) per day is sufficient for most dogs, although you may need to adjust the amount based on your dog's individual needs. 

When introducing a new food topper, start with a small amount and closely monitor your dog's reaction. If you notice any adverse effects, such as diarrhea or vomiting, discontinue use and consult your veterinarian. Gradually increase the amount over time, allowing your dog's digestive system to adjust to the new ingredient. 


### Can food toppers replace a dog's regular food?

Food toppers are not meant to replace a dog's regular food but rather to supplement their diet. A well-balanced dog food provides all the necessary nutrients for your dog's overall health and growth. Adding food toppers can enhance the quality of your dog's meals and address specific dietary needs, but it should not be the sole source of nutrition. 

Ensure that your dog receives a complete and balanced diet by combining a high-quality dog food with appropriate food toppers. Always consult with your veterinarian before making significant changes to your dog's diet, as they can provide personalized advice and recommendations based on your dog's unique needs and health. 


### What are the best food toppers for dogs?

Choosing the best food topper for your dog depends on your dog's specific needs and preferences. Some popular food toppers for dogs include freeze-dried raw meat, bone broth, pumpkin, sardines, and canned pumpkin. These toppers are high in protein, fiber, and essential vitamins and minerals, and can help improve your dog's overall health and wellbeing. 

When selecting a food topper, choose one that is made from natural, high-quality ingredients and is specifically formulated for canine consumption. Avoid any products with artificial preservatives, colors, or additives that may harm your dog's health. Always consult with your veterinarian before introducing a new food topper to ensure it's safe and appropriate for your dog's individual needs.
