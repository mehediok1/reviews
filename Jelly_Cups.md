### Important Features

When shopping for jelly cups, there are a few important features to consider. First, think about the material the jelly cups are made from. Most jelly cups are made from plastic or silicone, both of which are food-safe materials. While plastic may be more affordable, silicone jelly cups are more durable and can withstand hot and cold temperatures. 


### Size and Shape

Jelly cups come in various sizes and shapes. The most common size is about 1-2 inches in diameter and 1-2 inches deep. Some jelly cups have a flat bottom, while others have a pointed bottom. The size and shape of the jelly cup will depend on how you plan to serve the jelly and the presentation you want to achieve. 


### Flavors

The flavor options for jelly cups are endless! Some common flavors include strawberry, raspberry, grape, and orange. Jelly cups can also be filled with fruit preserves, marmalade, or even chocolate spreads. Consider the preferences of your guests when choosing the flavors for your jelly cups. Also, remember to check the ingredients to ensure they are allergy-friendly if needed. 


### Considerations and General Advice

When purchasing jelly cups, consider the quantity you will need based on the number of guests attending your event. It is always better to have more jelly cups than you need, so consider getting a few extra just in case. Also, think about storage and transportation of the jelly cups. They should be stored in an airtight container and kept in a cool, dry place.

### What materials are jelly cups made of?

Jelly cups are typically made from polypropylene, a type of plastic that is safe for food and drink consumption. Polypropylene jelly cups are designed to withstand various temperature changes, making them an excellent choice for hot and cold treats alike. The high-quality materials used in the production of jelly cups also make them durable and reusable, depending on the specific brand or manufacturer. ### How can jelly cups be stored and cleaned?

Storing jelly cups is a piece of cake since they can be stored in any pantry, refrigerator, or cupboard. It is essential to keep them in a cool, dry, and dark place to prevent the degradation of the plastic and maintain the integrity of the contents. Depending on the brand, some jelly cups may come with an airtight seal, while others may have a resealable lid. Make sure to secure the lid tightly after using the container to maintain its freshness and extend the shelf life of your treats. 

Cleaning jelly cups is another easy task since they are generally dishwasher safe. If you prefer to wash them by hand, it is best to use warm water and mild soap to remove any stains or residue. Do not scrub aggressively or use abrasive cleaning products, as they can damage the plastic surface and make it look dull or scratched. After washing, allow the cups to air dry before using them to avoid any water retention or mold growth.
