## FAQ


### What is a split flange?

A split flange is a type of mechanical flange used in piping systems. It is designed to provide a connection between two pipes or between a pipe and a valve, tank, or other equipment. A split flange is typically made of metal, such as stainless steel or carbon steel, and has a grooved shape that allows it to be easily assembled and disassembled. 

The split flange consists of two halves that can be bolted together. Each half has a flat surface that faces the other half when assembled, creating a seal that prevents leaks. The bolt holes in the flange are usually drilled in a staggered pattern, which helps prevent the flange from becoming misaligned when tightened, ensuring a proper seal. 


### What are the different types of split flanges?

There are several types of split flanges, including the following: 
1) weld-neck split flange, 2) slip-on split flange, and 3) socket weld split flange. Each type is designed for different applications and has specific features that make it more suitable for certain uses than others. For example, weld-neck split flanges are used in high-pressure applications, slip-on split flanges are used for lower pressure applications, and socket weld split flanges are used for applications where the flange needs to be welded to the pipe. 

The choice of which type of split flange to use depends on the requirements of the specific application, such as the pressure rating, material compatibility, and any specific design requirements. It is essential to consult with an expert or the manufacturer to determine the appropriate type of split flange for a given application.
