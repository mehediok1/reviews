## FAQ


### What are Yard Cards?

Yard Cards are a type of card game played outdoors, typically on a patio or backyard. They are similar in gameplay to well-known card games such as Solitaire and Spider, but have unique challenges that reflect their outdoor environment. Yard Cards often feature larger cards and larger numbers, making them easier to shuffle and deal. 

Yard Cards can be customizable, allowing players to choose themes, colors, and patterns that fit their personal style or match the outdoor space. Some popular themes include flowers, animals, and seasonal designs. The games themselves typically require players to make matches, either by stacking cards of the same rank or suit, or by forming sets of certain shapes or patterns. 


### Can Yard Cards be played by children?

Yes, Yard Cards are a family-friendly option suitable for all age groups. The larger cards and bigger game layout make it easier for children to understand the game mechanics and participate in a fun, engaging activity with their family and friends. Additionally, many Yard Cards themes include popular children's characters, adding an extra element of enjoyment for kids. 

For younger children who may not be familiar with standard card games, there are simplified versions of Yard Cards available, such as those that focus on matching colors or shapes. As children grow and gain more experience with cards and games, they can experiment with more complex versions of Yard Cards to continue challenging and entertaining themselves. 


### Will Yard Cards hold up to outdoor elements?

To protect Yard Cards from damaging environmental factors, manufacturers often use materials specifically designed for outdoor use. These materials can include waterproof laminate, UV-resistant materials, or durable plastic, ensuring that the cards will withstand exposure to various elements such as sun, rain, and wind without fading, bending, or breaking. 

Additionally, Yard Cards designed for outdoor use are constructed to withstand frequent handling and shuffling. They are typically designed to handle the rough-and-tumble nature of playing games outdoors, making them a more resilient and long-lasting option compared to indoor card games that are not intended for outdoor play.

These resources are more expensive than the ones typically used for indoor card games, such as standard cardstock. Additionally, the large size and higher quantity of cards in a Yard Card set can also inflate the price compared to smaller, regular card decks. 

However, owning and playing Yard Cards can still be a cost-effective and fun option, especially considering the extended outdoor enjoyment and durability provided by these games. With proper care and storage, a Yard Card set can be enjoyed in various outdoor settings for years to come, making the higher price well worth the investment in terms of enjoyment and longevity.
