## FAQ


### What is synthetic paper?

Synthetic paper, also known as synthetic parchment, is a man-made alternative to traditional natural parchment. It is made from various materials such as polyethylene, polypropylene, or polyvinyl alcohol, and is designed to mimic the feel and properties of natural parchment. Synthetic paper is widely used in various industries, including packaging, food, and pharmaceuticals, due to its durability, flexibility, and resistance to moisture and grease. 

Compared to natural parchment, synthetic paper offers several advantages, such as being more cost-effective, environmentally friendly, and easier to recycle. Additionally, synthetic paper can be produced in various sizes and thicknesses to cater to different applications and industries, making it a versatile alternative to traditional parchment.

This resistance also prevents the risk of spoilage and contamination. 

Thirdly, synthetic paper is more durable and flexible than natural parchment. ### How is synthetic paper made?

Synthetic paper is made from a wide variety of materials, such as polyethylene, polypropylene, or polyvinyl alcohol. These materials are melted, extruded, and then cooled and rolled to create a thin, flat sheet. The resulting sheet is then coated with a polymer to create a smooth, non-porous surface that mimics the feel and properties of natural parchment. 

The manufacturing process of synthetic paper allows for a wide range of sizes, thicknesses, and finishes to be produced, making it a versatile alternative to traditional parchment. Additionally, the use of synthetic materials ensures that the paper is more durable and flexible, with a higher resistance to moisture and grease, making it ideal for use in various industries.
