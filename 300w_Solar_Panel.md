### What is the difference between monocrystalline and polycrystalline solar panels?

Monocrystalline and polycrystalline solar panels are the two most common types of solar panels used in residential and commercial applications. The primary difference between these two types lies in their production methods and appearance. However, there are some practical differences in terms of efficiency, cost, and durability. 

Monocrystalline solar panels, also known as single crystal solar panels, are made from a single silicon crystal. The manufacturing process is more involved, making these panels more expensive but also more efficient, typically around 15-20% more efficient than polycrystalline solar panels. The appearance of monocrystalline solar panels is a uniform, dark blue color. These panels are generally more durable and offer higher performance in low-light conditions compared to polycrystalline solar panels.
