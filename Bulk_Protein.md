### Important Features to Consider

When you're shopping for bulk protein powder, there are several key features to think about. These include protein source, protein content, flavor and taste, and any additional ingredients or additives. Let's take a closer look at each one of these factors in turn, and what you should look for when making your buying decision. 


### Protein Source

The first thing to consider when buying bulk protein is the source of the protein. Most protein powders come from whey, soy, casein, pea, or egg. If you're looking for a protein supplement designed to build muscle, whey protein is a popular choice, as it contains all the essential amino acids and has a high biological value. If you follow a vegan or vegetarian diet, you may prefer a protein powder derived from plant sources such as pea or soy. Casein and egg proteins are also worth considering, as they have a slower absorption rate, making them a good option for before bedtime or to help you stay full for longer. 


### Protein Content

Another key consideration when buying bulk protein supplements is the amount of protein per serving. This will vary from brand to brand and product to product, so it's important to check the nutrition label and compare the different options. A general guideline is that a high-quality protein supplement should contain at least 20 grams of protein per serving. 


### Flavor and Taste

When you're taking a bulk protein supplement, it's important that you enjoy the taste, as you'll likely be drinking it on a regular basis. Most protein powders come in a range of flavors, from classic chocolate and vanilla to more unusual options like cookies and cream, strawberry banana, and salted caramel. If you're sensitive to certain flavors or prefer a natural taste, you can look for products that are available in unflavored or unsweetened varieties.
