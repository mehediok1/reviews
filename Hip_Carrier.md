### How do I clean and maintain my Hip Carrier?

To ensure the longevity and cleanliness of your Hip Carrier, it is crucial to follow the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning and maintaining the product. Typically, hand washing the carrier using mild detergent and cold water is recommended. To remove any stubborn stains, use a sponge or soft brush, avoiding abrasive materials to avoid damaging the fabric. 

After cleaning, air-dry the carrier away from direct sunlight to prevent color fading or losing its shape. Store your hip carrier in a clean, dry place, away from humidity and heat. Regularly inspect the carrier for any signs of wear and tear and replace any damaged parts to maintain its safe and efficient functionality. 


### How can I tell if my Hip Carrier fits correctly?

A properly fitted hip carrier is crucial for both comfort and safety. The carrier should be snug around the parent's waist and hips, but not too tight that it restricts movement. Adjustable straps will ensure proper fitting. The shoulder straps should be short enough to prevent slipping but long enough not to be placed too low onto the shoulders. 

When attaching the baby, their legs should be supported so that their knees bend slightly and are above their hips. Make sure the baby is facing you with their head close to your chest and their back straight. Adjust the carrier as necessary to create a secure and comfortable position for both parent and baby.
