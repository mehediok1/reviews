### Important Features to Consider

Before diving into specific details, it's crucial to identify the core features that make sketch paper unique. These include the paper type, texture, thickness, and bleed resistance. Sketch paper is typically made from high-quality materials that cater to artists who appreciate smooth, durable surfaces for their drawings. 

![Sketch-Paper-2](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Sketch-Paper-2/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Texture

The texture of sketch paper plays a vital role in determining its usability. Most sketch papers come with a slight tooth or texture, which helps to hold the graphite or pencil marks without smudging. A smoother texture may be suitable for those who prefer finer or lighter lines, while a slightly rougher texture might be best for those who love bold, expressive strokes. 


### Thickness

Sketch paper is usually available in different thicknesses, such as 40, 60, 90, or 120 GSM. The GSM (grams per square meter) rating indicates the weight of the paper. Heavier paper tends to provide better durability and resistance to bleeding or smudging. However, it may not be ideal for those who prefer a lighter feel or wish to work on larger canvases. Consider the weight of the paper that best suits your drawing style and projects. 

![Sketch-Paper-3](https://imagedelivery.net/vy2bglCGN6hEeWOnSe2c7A/Sketch-Paper-3/h=540,fit=pad,background=black)


### Bleed Resistance

Bleed resistance is another crucial aspect when it comes to sketch paper. The bleed resistance of sketch paper refers to its ability to prevent ink from bleeding through the paper. If you're someone who uses water-based markers, pens, or ink, look for sketch paper with excellent bleed resistance. High-quality sketch papers often feature a coating on the backside that prevents ink from seeping through.
