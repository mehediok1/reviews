### Size and Shape

The size and shape of the rocks you choose will have a significant impact on the overall look of your landscaping project. Larger rocks may be more suitable for larger spaces, while smaller rocks can be used to fill in gaps and add detail. Shape can also help create different visual effects – rounded rocks can add a softer, more organic feel, while angular rocks can create a more modern or sculptural look. 


### Material

Large landscape rocks are available in a variety of materials, such as granite, basalt, limestone, and slate. Each material has its unique characteristics, which can affect the durability, weight, and appearance of the rocks. Granite and basalt are common choices due to their durability and natural beauty. Limestone and slate offer a softer, more textured appearance but may require more maintenance to prevent erosion. Consider the climate and local environmental factors when choosing a material for your landscape rocks, as well as your personal preferences and budget. 


### Weight and Maintenance

Large landscape rocks can be heavy, so it's essential to choose rocks that are appropriate for your space and your ability to handle and move them. Consider the weight capacity of your garden beds or other structures where the rocks will be placed. It's also essential to think about the ongoing maintenance of your rocks. Some materials may need to be sealed or treated to prevent erosion or weathering. Be sure to research the care requirements of the rocks you're interested in to ensure they will look great for years to come.

Large landscape rocks can be incorporated into your landscaping project in various ways. You can also use rocks to create a water feature, such as a pond or waterfall, or to build a seating area or fire pit.
