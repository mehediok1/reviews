### Material

The material your Armor Helmet is made from is one of the most important factors to consider. High-quality helmets are typically crafted from materials like Kevlar, carbon fiber, or a combination of both. These materials provide excellent strength, durability, and resistance to impact, ensuring your head stays protected during battles. When shopping for an Armor Helmet, always prioritize materials that offer optimal protection and performance.

### Protection Level

Armor Helmets come with different protection levels, which are rated according to their ability to absorb and deflect both ballistic and shrapnel damage. Generally, helmets are classified into three categories: Level IIIA, Level III, and Level IV. Level IIIA helmets provide basic protection against handgun rounds and some rifle rounds, while Level III helmets offer more extensive coverage against rifle rounds. Level IV helmets, on the other hand, are designed to withstand armor-piercing rounds and provide the most advanced protection for your head. When selecting a helmet, choose one that offers the protection level that best suits your intended use and risk factors. 


### Fit and Comfort

An ill-fitting Armor Helmet can be uncomfortable, hinder your vision, and potentially compromise your safety. That's why it's essential to consider a helmet's fit and comfort features. Look for helmets with adjustable straps and padding that allow for a customized fit, as well as minimal weight to reduce fatigue. Ensure that the helmet allows for proper ventilation and doesn't restrict your movement, especially around your face and jaw. Always try on a helmet before purchasing or investing in an adjustment service to ensure the best fit possible.

While the primary function of an Armor Helmet is to protect your head, additional features can enhance its performance and convenience. Communication systems, such as built-in radios or headband-mounted earphones, can help keep you connected with your team during battles. Detachable face shields or NVG compatibility make it easier to see in various lighting conditions or when wearing night vision gear. When choosing an Armor Helmet, consider these value-added features and how they can benefit you on the battlefield.
