### Types of Unflavored Whey Protein

There are several types of unflavored whey protein, including whey protein concentrate (WPC), whey protein isolate (WPI), and hydrolyzed whey protein. Each type has its own benefits and drawbacks, so it's essential to understand the differences before making a purchase. WPC is the most common type and is less expensive than WPI, but it contains more lactose and fats. WPI is more expensive, but it contains less lactose and fats and is more quickly absorbed by the body. Hydrolyzed whey protein is the most expensive option, but it is highly soluble and easily digestible, making it suitable for those with lactose intolerance or digestive issues.

## FAQ


### What is unflavored whey protein made of?

Unflavored whey protein is made from whey, a liquid byproduct of milk that is separated and processed during cheese production. The product is a popular source of high-quality protein, which can help support muscle growth and repair. The unflavored aspect ensures that the product has no added flavors and can be used in various recipes without altering the taste. 

It is important to note that whey protein products can be derived from cow's milk or other sources like soy. The protein obtained from these sources may differ slightly in amino acid composition; however, they provide similar benefits for muscle growth and recovery. Check the product label to confirm the source of your preferred whey protein. 


### What is the difference between flavored and unflavored whey protein?

The main difference between flavored and unflavored whey protein lies in the addition of artificial or natural flavors, colors, and sweeteners to improve the taste. Flavored whey protein usually comes in a variety of flavors like chocolate, vanilla, strawberry, and others, while unflavored protein has a neutral taste that doesn't interfere with the flavors of recipes it is added to. 

Aside from the taste, flavored whey protein products might contain additional ingredients, such as added vitamins and minerals. Unflavored protein, on the other hand, has fewer additives and is a better choice for those who prefer to consume protein without any added flavors or sweeteners. However, both unflavored and flavored protein products are suitable options depending on an individual's taste preferences and requirements. 


### How much protein should I consume daily?

Recommended daily protein intake varies based on factors such as age, gender, body weight, and activity level. The popularly recommended value is approximately 0.8 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight daily. However, this number can be adjusted based on individual needs, such as athletes, bodybuilders, or those going through weight loss or muscle gain programs. 

It is always best to consult a healthcare professional or a registered dietitian to determine the appropriate amount of protein needed to meet your specific goals and health requirements. Taking an appropriate serving of unflavored whey protein daily can help support optimal muscle growth and recovery.
