There are two main types of push lights: LED and Halogen. Both types have their advantages and disadvantages, and the choice between them depends on your preferences and requirements. LED push lights are energy-efficient, long-lasting, and offer a wide range of light colors and color temperature options. Halogen push lights, on the other hand, provide a warm, traditional glow but consume more energy and have a shorter lifespan. 


### Size and Shape

Push lights come in various sizes and shapes, ranging from small, compact designs to larger, more decorative options. Consider the space where the light will be installed and choose a size and shape that complements the room aesthetically. Larger push lights can be more decorative, while smaller ones are ideal for task lighting and discreet placement. 


### Installation

Push lights can be installed in several ways, including wired, battery-powered, or using an extension cord. Wired push lights require professional installation and are more suitable for permanent fixtures. Battery-powered and corded push lights are more convenient for temporary installations or areas where wiring is not possible. Make sure to consider the installation requirements and your personal preferences when choosing a push light. 


### Adhesive and Mounting Hardware

The type of adhesive and mounting hardware included with the push light is essential for secure installation and ensuring the light stays in place. Common adhesives include double-sided tape, silicone, and quick-setting epoxy. Choose a product with a reliable adhesive and strong mounting hardware to ensure your push light stays in place, even in high-moisture or high-temperature environments.

Some push lights have a hinged or pivoting mechanism, allowing the user to adjust the light direction and brightness easily. 

Once installed, the push light can be dimmed or switched on/off through a central control system or by using a local switch. The light emitted is directed at the surface or object that needs illumination, providing targeted and efficient lighting.
