### What is the purpose of hardside carry on luggage?

Hardside carry on luggage is designed to provide durability, protection, and style for your travel essentials. These luggage pieces generally have a rigid shell made of materials like polycarbonate, aluminum, or ABS and are typically wheeled, making it easier to transport your items efficiently through airports and other crowded places. 

Hardside carry on luggage is ideal for those who travel frequently and need their luggage to withstand wear and tear from constant usage. It also helps safeguard your belongings from potential theft and damage during transit, while still being compact enough to fit under the seat or in an overhead bin on most flights. 


### What are the benefits of hardside luggage compared to softside luggage?

Hardside luggage offers several advantages over softside options. Firstly, it is generally more durable and resistant to scratches, dents, and impacts. Secondly, the exterior design can give a sleeker and more professional look, which can be beneficial for business travelers. Finally, hardside luggage typically offers better organization and compartmentalization, with features like built-in pockets, tie-down straps, and multiple compartments. 

However, some drawbacks of hardside luggage include its weight and potential difficulty in fitting into tight spaces, such as overhead bins. Softside luggage might be a better choice for those who prioritize maneuverability and flexibility. Ultimately, the decision between hardside and softside luggage depends on individual needs and preferences.

### What are the most common materials used in hardside luggage?

The most common materials used in hardside luggage are polycarbonate, ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene) plastic, and aluminum. Each material has its own unique properties and benefits, which might influence your choice of luggage, including durability, weight, and cost. Polycarbonate is a strong, lightweight material known for its durability and resilience. ABS plastic is typically lightweight and cost-effective, while aluminum is often more expensive but highly resistant to wear and tear.
