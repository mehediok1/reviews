## FAQ


### What is a Tea Holder?

A Tea Holder, also known as a Tea Infuser, is a tool designed specifically to hold and steep tea leaves within a cup or teapot. It allows users to easily brew and infuse their favorite teas without the need for loose leaf tea filtration. Most tea holders consist of a small, handcrafted wire or silicone cage that securely holds the tea leaves in place while water flows through them, extracting the tea's flavor and aroma. 

Tea Holders are essential for both traditional and modern tea drinkers, offering a convenient and practical solution for enjoying hot, fragrant brews from the comfort of home or on-the-go. These versatile accessories are made in a variety of materials and styles, allowing users to select the one that best matches their tea preferences and aesthetic preferences. 


### How do I use a Tea Holder?

Using a Tea Holder is easy and straightforward. To brew tea using a Tea Holder, simply follow these steps: 
1. Begin by selecting your desired tea leaves or tea bag. 2. Place the tea leaves or tea bag inside the Tea Holder's cage, ensuring they are situated securely. 3. Fill a cup or teapot with hot water, taking care not to pour water directly on the tea leaves or holder. 4. Insert the Tea Holder into the cup or teapot, ensuring it is properly aligned and sits stably. 5. Allow the tea to steep for the recommended time ‚Äì typically 3-5 minutes for black tea, 2-4 minutes for green tea, and 3-5 minutes for herbal teas. 6. Remove the Tea Holder from the cup or teapot once the desired steeping time has elapsed. 7. Enjoy your freshly brewed tea, either directly from the cup or by pouring it into a separate tea cup. 

It is essential to rinse your Tea Holder after each use, removing any excess tea leaves that may have accumulated. This will help maintain the cleanliness and longevity of your Tea Holder, as well as ensure a consistent level of flavor in your brewed tea. Some Tea Holders can be easily cleaned with hot soapy water, while others may require a specific cleaning regimen to maintain their integrity and appearance.

### Are Tea Holders dishwasher safe?

Not all Tea Holders are dishwasher safe, as their materials and construction can vary. Before attempting to clean your Tea Holder in a dishwasher, ensure that it is specifically labeled as dishwasher-safe by the manufacturer. In many cases, it is recommendable to hand-wash your Tea Holder to preserve its integrity and appearance. 

To hand-wash a Tea Holder, fill a basin or sink with hot soapy water. Gently agitate the Tea Holder, ensuring that any tea leaves or debris are removed. Rinse thoroughly with warm water to remove soap residue. If necessary, you can use a soft-bristled brush or toothbrush to reach into tight spaces or small crevices for a thorough cleaning. Always dry your Tea Holder promptly to prevent any rusting or discoloration of metal materials. Many manufacturers suggest storing your Tea Holder in a clean, dry place when not in use to ensure optimal performance and longevity.

Yes, Tea Holders are designed to be used within a variety of tea containers, including teapots with infuser mechanisms. When selecting a Tea Holder for use with a teapot, ensure that it is compatible with the specific teapot in terms of size and strainer opening. Many Tea Holders come with adjustable or interchangeable components that allow for customization based on the size and shape of your teapot and desired tea leaves. 

When using a Tea Holder in a teapot, make sure the holder is properly aligned and sits stably within the teapot's strainer. Allow the tea to steep according to the recommended time for your chosen tea variety. Once the steeping time has elapsed, remove the Tea Holder from the teapot and enjoy your freshly brewed tea with friends or family. 


### What is the difference between a Tea Holder and a Tea Infuser?

Although the terms "Tea Holder" and "Tea Infuser" are sometimes used interchangeably, there are subtle differences between these two types of tea accessories. A Tea Infuser typically refers to a larger, more robust wire or mesh container specifically designed for use with traditional tea pots or infusers. These infusers usually possess a wider opening and handle, providing ease of use and stability when steeped directly in a pot or teapot. 

On the other hand, a Tea Holder is generally a smaller, more compact wire or silicone cage that fits securely within a cup or individual tea glass. While some Tea Infusers can be used with cups or glasses, they are primarily known for their use with more substantial tea pots or decanters. Depending on your tea-drinking preferences, choosing between a Tea Holder and a Tea Infuser may come down to personal preference, the type of tea you plan to brew, your preferred brewing method, and the specific tea accessories you have on hand.
