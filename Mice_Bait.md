## FAQ


### How does mice bait work?

Mice bait works by attracting mice through the scent of the bait. Typically, it contains food-like ingredients such as peanut butter, cheese, or chocolate. The mice are drawn towards the food smell. Once the mouse bites into the bait, it becomes trapped, as the bait has a chemical or sticky substance to immobilize or kill the mouse. 

There are two main types of mice bait: live capture and lethal. Live capture baits trap the mice and allow you to release them elsewhere without harm. Lethal baits either poison the mice or immobilize them for a long enough time to allow you to dispose of them safely. The choice between these two types depends on personal preference and the specific situation. 


### What is the best mice bait?

The best mice bait is subjective and depends on various factors like the type of infestation, location, and preference for capture or lethal control. In general, lethal baits that combine a palatable food source with a fast-acting poison like warfarin or brodifacoum are often recommended for their effectiveness in controlling mice populations. 

For live capture baits, look for baits that are easy to set up, trap the mice, and hold them securely. Consider products that are user-friendly, durable, and provide a good balance between trap size and mice size. Some also come with additional features like a safety release mechanism to ensure the wellbeing of the trapped mice. 


### Is it safe to use mice bait around children and pets?

It is not safe to use mice bait around children and pets. The bait contains chemicals or substances that can be toxic to humans and animals. Children may accidentally ingest mice bait, and pets, especially if they are curious or hungry, could also be at risk. To protect your family and pet, it is crucial to keep mice bait out of reach and use alternative pest control methods if necessary. 

Some non-toxic options include traps designed specifically for children and their pets or even ultrasonic devices that deter rodents without using chemicals. Alternatively, you can hire a professional pest control service to identify and address the problem safely in your home or workplace. Remember, it is always better to prioritize the safety of those who share your home with you. 


### How long does mice bait take to work?

The effectiveness of mice bait can vary, depending on the type of bait used, the amount of bait placed, and various environmental factors such as temperature, humidity, and the presence of competing food sources. Generally, it takes a few days to a couple of weeks for the mice bait to start producing results. 

If you're dealing with a significant mice infestation, it may take some time to notice a decline in the number of mice activity. However, if you're consistently placing mice bait according to the product's instructions and noticing an increase in the number of trapped mice, it indicates that the bait is effective. 


### Does mice bait have an expiration date?

Most mice baits do not have an explicit expiration date. However, it is essential to store the product in a cool, dry place away from heat and moisture. Exposure to these factors can degrade the quality and effectiveness of the bait over time. Check the packaging for any specific storage instructions or best-before date guidelines. 

Ensure you rotate your bait regularly to ensure that it remains fresh and effective. It is also a good idea to use mice bait that has been stored properly to prevent wastage and maintain effectiveness. For a longer shelf life, some products provide a recommended storage period, so follow the instructions on the packaging. 


### Can mice bait attract other pests?

In general, mice baits are designed to attract mice due to their scent and ingredients. However, there is a risk of unintended consequences, such as attracting other pests. This can occur if the bait is not stored properly, if the bait is left exposed for a long time, or if the bait is too palatable for other pests. 

To minimize the risk of attracting other pests, follow the storage guidelines on the packaging, use the bait within a reasonable timeframe, and keep it away from areas that are easily accessible to other animals. Additionally, choose a product with less attractive scents for non-target pests. By following these precautions, you can reduce the risks associated with using mice baits around your home or workplace.
