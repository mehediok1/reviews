### How does Glow In The Dark Body Paint work?

Glow In The Dark Body Paint contains special pigments called "phosphors" that are specifically designed to absorb and store energy from UV light or sunlight. When these phosphors are heated or exposed to UV light, they release the stored energy in the form of visible light, creating a stunning glow effect on the skin. 

The intensity and duration of the glow depend on several factors, including the concentration of phosphors in the paint, the UV light source, and the amount of sunlight exposure. In general, the glow lasts for several hours, depending on the factors mentioned earlier. To maintain the glow effect for as long as possible, it is recommended to avoid washing the body paint off too quickly and to reapply it if necessary. 


### What is the difference between Glow In The Dark Body Paint and regular body paint?

The main difference between Glow In The Dark Body Paint and regular body paint is the presence of phosphors in the former. These phosphors are responsible for the glow effect when exposed to UV light or sunlight. Regular body paints do not contain these phosphors and, therefore, cannot emit light when exposed to those light sources. 

Additionally, Glow In The Dark Body Paint has a slightly different consistency than regular body paint, making it easier to apply an even coat of color on the skin.
