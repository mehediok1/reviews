## FAQ


### What are semi cured gel nails?

Semi cured gel nails are a type of gel polish that has been cured for a shorter amount of time compared to fully cured gel nails. The curing process typically involves placing the nails under a UV or LED lamp for a shorter duration, resulting in a slightly more flexible and less durable finish than fully cured gel nails. 

Despite being less durable, semi cured gel nails can still last up to two weeks when properly applied and maintained. They are suitable for those who want a quicker gel polish application or prefer a softer feel on their nails compared to fully cured gel nails. Semi cured gel nails are also easier to remove than fully cured gel nails, making them a more convenient choice for some users. 


### What are the benefits of using semi cured gel nails?

The main benefits of semi cured gel nails are their easier removal process and more flexible feel compared to fully cured gel nails. Semi cured gel nails can be removed easily within a few days using standard gel nail removal techniques, while fully cured gel nails can be more difficult and time-consuming to remove. Additionally, the semi cured gel nails provide a softer feel on the nails, which some people may find more comfortable. 

Moreover, the shorter curing time for semi cured gel nails makes them a more convenient option for those who are short on time, don't have access to a UV or LED lamp, or want a quick touch-up when on the go. Overall, semi cured gel nails are a versatile option for those looking for a balance of convenience and durability in their gel polish. 


### How are semi cured gel nails applied?

Applying semi cured gel nails follows a similar process to applying fully cured gel nails. First, the natural nails must be prepared by removing any excess polish, oil, and moisture to ensure proper adherence. Then, a base coat is applied to the nail to create a smooth surface for the gel polish to adhere to. After that, the semi cured gel polish is applied to the nail, and a UV or LED lamp is used to cure it for a shorter duration (usually around 30 seconds to 2 minutes) compared to fully cured gel nails (usually around 30 seconds to 2 minutes). 

Once the curing process is complete, the gel polish should be cured for an additional 30 seconds to 2 minutes under the lamp to ensure optimal hardness and durability. The final step involves curing the nail with the lamp for 1-2 minutes to create a protective seal for the nail. After the application process is complete, the nails should be cleaned, polished, and allowed to air dry fully. Semi cured gel nails can be easily touched up if needed, simply by curing the nail under a lamp for a short period. 


### What are the drawbacks of using semi cured gel nails?

The main drawback of using semi cured gel nails is that they are less durable than fully cured gel nails. Since they are not cured for as long, they may chip, peel, or scratch more easily, leading to a shorter wear time. On average, semi cured gel nails can last up to two weeks, while fully cured gel nails can last up to three weeks or more. 

Moreover, semi cured gel nails are less resistant to staining and water exposure as compared to fully cured gel nails. They are more likely to get chipped or peeled off if exposed to water or chemicals, such as nail polish remover or cleaning products. Proper care and maintenance are essential to extend the lifespan of semi cured gel nails. 


### How can I remove semi cured gel nails safely?

Removing semi cured gel nails is a relatively easy process, but it should be done carefully to avoid damaging the natural nails. Start by soaking your nails in warm, soapy water for 10-15 minutes to soften the gel polish. Then, use a cuticle pusher or orangewood stick to gently lift the edge of the gel polish off the natural nail. Be sure not to push too hard to prevent any damage to the nails. 

Once the gel polish is lifted, it can be gently filed away using a nail file or a metal nail buffer. Avoid using harsh chemicals like acetone or nail polish remover, as they can damage the natural nails and cause them to become brittle. After the semi cured gel nails are removed, your nails should be cleaned and moisturized to maintain their health and strength.
