## FAQ


### What is double-sided tape used for?

Double-sided tape is commonly used for a variety of purposes, including mounting and attaching posters and prints, fixing carpets, binding books, and creating temporary labels on packages. It's also useful for securing small items, holding paper documents together or organizing cables and wires, making it a versatile product for personal or professional use. 

One of its primary uses is in the world of interior design and home renovation, where it is used to secure furniture, wallpaper, and other decorative elements. Double-sided tape is also popular in the construction industry for attaching blueprints and other documents in building sites, making it easier for workers to refer to them. 


### What are the most common types of double-sided tape?

Certainly, there are numerous types of double-sided tape available in the market. Common types include low-tack, medium-tack, and high-tack adhesives, which vary based on the strength needed for the materials they're attached to. Other materials include acrylic, rubber, silicone, and even foam tapes, each with its own unique features and applications. 

For instance, low-tack double-sided tape is generally used for temporary applications that won't damage the surfaces they're attached to or require easy removal, such as holding up posters. On the other hand, medium-tack or high-tack adhesives are more durable and ideal for use with materials like wallpaper, wood, and stone, where they provide better long-term adhesion. 


### How do I apply double-sided tape correctly?

Applying double-sided tape is quite simple. First, always clean the surface you wish to attach it to, removing any dirt, oils, or residue. This will help ensure that the tape adheres to the surface correctly. Next, you can decide where you want to place the tape. Depending on the tape's strength, you may need to overlap the tape slightly for better security. 

Once you've decided on the desired application, remove the liner from the tape, exposing one side with the adhesive. Gently press it onto the clean, dry surface, smoothing out all air bubbles. Allow the tape to set, ensuring it's fully adhered to the surface. After that, carefully apply your piece or document onto the tape to finalize the connection. 


### Is double-sided tape reusable?

When it comes to reusability, it generally depends on the specific type of double-sided tape you're using. Many adhesives are not reusable, especially once they've contacted the surface they're intended to bond with. This is because, during the initial application, the adhesive molecules penetrate into the small microscopic pores on the surface. 

However, some tapes such as foam tapes and removable adhesive tapes can be repositionable or reusable, especially if they've not bonded with the surface properly or have been partially detached. As with any double-sided tape, it's always best to refer to the manufacturer's guidelines for specific usage guidelines and requirements. 


### Can I use double-sided tape outdoors?

While double-sided tape is excellent for indoor applications, using it outdoors can be a potential issue due to the elements such as moisture, extreme temperatures, or UV rays. Some types of double-sided tape are specifically designed for outdoor usage, like weather-proof tapes, which can hold up against varying weather conditions and different types of surfaces.

### How do I remove double-sided tape without damaging the surface?

Removing double-sided tape from surfaces can be tricky, especially if the adhesive has already bonded with the material. However, there are several methods you can try without disturbing the surface. One such method is using a plastic scraper, which can gently lift the tape off without leaving any residue behind. 

For those looking for a more gentle method, you can try heat, moisture, or a combination of both. Using a hairdryer or a heated cloth on the tape for a minute or two can soften the adhesive, making it easier to peel off. Also, applying a little bit of vinegar or isopropyl alcohol to a cloth before wiping between the tape and the surface can help break down the adhesive. Just be sure to test it on a small area first.
