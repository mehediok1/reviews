## FAQ


### What is a purple cleaner?

A purple cleaner is a type of cleaning product that uses dye to give an attractive appearance. This color differentiation helps users identify and organize different cleaning products more easily, which can make it easier to use the right product for the right task. Purple cleaning products typically contain a combination of powerful cleaning agents and fragrances to leave surfaces clean and smelling fresh. 

The purple color is not just for aesthetic purposes; it may also contain certain dyes that function as a preservative or biocide. This helps prolong the product's shelf life and enhance its effectiveness in fighting off mold, mildew, and bacteria present on surfaces. It is essential to read the label and follow the usage instructions to ensure proper cleaning results and avoid damage to surfaces. 


### What types of surfaces are purple cleaners suitable for?

Purple cleaners are typically suitable for a variety of surfaces, such as floors, walls, countertops, and bathroom fixtures. They are versatile products that can be used in different areas of the house or outside to clean various types of grime, dirt, and stains left by foot traffic, pets, food spills, grease, and other household messes. Purple cleaners work well on non-porous surfaces like tiles, vinyl, and glass, as well as porous materials like carpets, grout, and fabrics. 

However, it is crucial to read the label and follow the manufacturer's recommendations for the specific product you are using, as some purple cleaners may not be safe for certain materials or may not be suitable for specific tasks. Always test a hidden or inconspicuous area before applying to ensure that the product will not damage the surface. It is also essential to rinse and clean surfaces well to prevent soap scum buildup and residues. 


### What precautions should I take while using a purple cleaner?

Using any cleaning product, including purple cleaners, requires precaution to ensure safety and maintain the integrity of the surfaces you are cleaning. First, read the label carefully for any safety information or warnings. This may include information about the potential for skin irritation or contact with the eyes, as well as any potential health hazards. 

Next, ensure that you are wearing proper personal protective equipment (PPE), such as gloves and safety goggles, to minimize the risk of chemical exposure. Avoid inhaling vapors or fumes and make sure to work in a well-ventilated area. Always follow the proper dilution and usage instructions specified on the product label. In case of accidental ingestion or contact with the skin or eyes, rinse the area thoroughly with water and seek medical attention if needed.

It is not recommended to use undiluted purple cleaner on surfaces. The concentrated formula may be too harsh and could potentially damage the surface or be hazardous to your health. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions on the product label regarding the appropriate dilution ratio for the specific product. 

Using the recommended dilution not only ensures proper cleaning results and prevents potential damage to the surface but also reduces the risk of accidental exposure to the concentrated product. In some cases, you may need to adjust the dilution rate based on the type of grime or stain you are trying to remove. If unsure, consult the product's user manual or customer support.
