## Buyer's Guide

A leaf guard for gutters is an essential accessory that can keep your home's exterior clean and dry. By preventing leaves, debris, and even pests from clogging your gutters, a leaf guard can save you time and money on costly repairs and maintenance. While there are various types of leaf guards available on the market, this buyer's guide will help you understand the most important features, considerations, and advice for selecting the right leaf guard for your gutters. 


### Types of Leaf Guards

There are four primary types of leaf guards that you might come across when shopping for one. The most common ones are the mesh, foam, brush, and gutter cover leaf guards. Each of these types has its own unique benefits and drawbacks, so it's essential to understand these differences to make an informed decision. 


### Mesh Leaf Guards

Mesh leaf guards are made from a perforated, flexible material that is often reinforced with a metal frame. These guards allow water to flow through the mesh while keeping out larger debris, such as leaves and twigs. Mesh leaf guards are an excellent choice for homes with tall trees or heavy foliage and can typically be installed without any specialized tools. 


### Foam Leaf Guards

Foam leaf guards use a porous, foam-like material that lets water pass through while blocking debris and pests. Installation is straightforward, but these guards are more prone to degradation over time and may need replacement after a few years of use. Foam leaf guards also tend to be the most budget-friendly option on the market. 


### Brush Leaf Guards

Brush leaf guards consist of a set of long, flexible bristles that create a tight seal along the edge of the gutter. These guards do an excellent job of keeping out debris but can be more challenging to install, as some special tools might be required. Additionally, brush guards are typically not as aesthetically pleasing as some of the other options on the market. 


## FAQ


### What is a gutter guard that keeps leaves from clogging?

A gutter guard designed for leaves is a safeguard that obstructs debris, such as leaves or twigs, from obstructing your gutters. These typically feature a barrier or mesh that only permits water to flow through your gutters, keeping out unwanted particles. Implementing this device facilitates the efficient performance of your gutter system, diminishing the regular cleaning requirement.

Generally, there are three types of gutter guards to choose from: snap-on covers, screw-on covers, and exterior gutter guards. The first is easily removable and snaps on your gutter using a clamping mechanism. Snap-on covers are versatile, fitting with different gutter materials and styles. Screw-on covers, on the other hand, offer a secure grip by attaching screws directly to the gutter. Although they might need professional installation, they provide a reliable barrier against debris. Lastly, exterior gutter guards are attached outside the gutter system, usually with adhesive or screws that fit over the gutter. They are perfect for those with tall or difficult-to-reach gutters as they become easier to clean. 

All types of gutter guards come with their pros and cons, so pick a guard based on your specific gutter style, needs, and budget. For instance, snap-on covers are budget-friendly, while screw-on covers offer a more secure grip to debris. 


### What benefits do leaf guards provide to gutters?

A gutter guard designed for leaves has several advantages: 
1) reduced maintenance: Since debris remains outside the gutters, cleaning becomes less frequent;

3) increased longevity: Gutter guards protect the gutters from blockages and debris, thereby prolonging their life span; and 4) safety: Reducing the need for ladders while cleaning gutters makes the process safer. 

Additionally, leaf guards can benefit older homes with tall gutters or those that are challenging to reach, making cleaning a more manageable task. Lastly, maintaining your home's appearance is easier with a cleaner and unclogged gutter system, contributing to the overall aesthetic of your property.

Gutter guards come in various types, each with its own unique installation process and maintenance requirements. The primary types include:
