## FAQ


### What is tequila made of?

Tequila is primarily made from the blue agave plant. The heart of this plant is harvested, cooked, and then mashed to extract the juice. This juice is then fermented and distilled, resulting in the tequila that we know and enjoy. Some tequilas may also contain additional flavors or ingredients, but the base ingredient is always the blue agave plant. 

It is essential to note that tequila is only considered genuine if it is produced in the state of Jalisco, Mexico, or in specific municipalities of other Mexican states, following strict regulations. Genuine tequila is often marked with the word "Tequila" on its label and may also bear a seal from the Mexican Tequila Regulatory Council. 


### What are some popular brands of tequila?

There are many popular brands of tequila, each with its unique flavor profile and characteristics. Some of the most well-known brands include Jose Cuervo, Don Julio, Patron, and Hornitos. These brands offer a range of tequilas, from silver (blanco) to reposado, aged, and even extra aged varieties. Some of these brands also produce special or limited edition tequilas, which can be a great gift for the tequila lover in your life. 

It is essential to taste different brands and types of tequila to determine which one suits your palate best. When it comes to choosing a tequila as a gift, consider the recipient's preferred style, such as a smoother, aged variety or a more robust, tequila that's perfect for making cocktails. You might also want to consider a tequila gift set, which could include various flavors, glasses, or even a tequila-making kit for the ultimate gift experience. 


### Can tequila be enjoyed with food?

Absolutely! Tequila is versatile and can be enjoyed in various ways, including with food. Some popular food pairings for tequila include spicy Mexican dishes, seafood, and grilled meats. Tequila also pairs well with a variety of fruits, such as lime, lemon, and grapefruit. This makes it an excellent choice for creating refreshing cocktails, margaritas, and other tequila-based drinks. 

When enjoying tequila with food, it is essential to consider the flavor profile of the tequila you choose. Lighter tequilas are great for pairing with seafood and lighter dishes, while more robust, aged varieties can complement richer, spicier foods. Additionally, experimenting with different tequila-based cocktails can lead to a variety of flavor combinations that can enhance your culinary experience. 


### How should I store tequila?

Tequila should be stored in a cool, dark place, away from direct sunlight. Ideally, it should be stored in a dark-colored glass bottle or a bottle with a dark-colored label, as this can help protect the tequila from exposure to light, which can affect its flavor and quality over time. Temperature fluctuations can also impact the quality of tequila, so it is best to keep it in a stable environment. 

Additionally, it is essential to avoid exposing tequila to extreme temperatures, such as direct sunlight or freezing temperatures. Storing tequila in a humid environment, such as a wine cellar, can also help maintain its quality and flavor. Proper storage will ensure that your tequila remains fresh and flavorful for a longer period. 


### Is tequila gluten-free?

Yes, tequila is generally considered gluten-free. The blue agave plant, from which tequila is made, does not contain gluten. However, some tequila products may contain additives, such as gluten-containing grains or colorings, that could potentially make them glutenous. It is essential to check the label of any tequila product you plan to consume or serve to ensure that it is gluten-free. 

Most reputable tequila brands take great care to avoid adding any gluten-containing ingredients to their products. When in doubt, it is always best to consult with the manufacturer or a knowledgeable professional for clarification on any specific concerns or questions you may have about tequila and gluten. 


### How do I serve tequila?

Tequila can be enjoyed in various ways, depending on your preference. One of the most common ways to serve tequila is straight, chilled, and in a shot glass. This allows the true flavor of the tequila to shine through, and it's an excellent choice for sipping tequila on its own or as an aperitif. Some people also prefer to serve tequila over ice or with a splash of water to smooth the flavor out. 

Tequila is also a popular ingredient in many cocktails, including margaritas, tequila sunrise, and the Paloma. When making cocktails, it is essential to use high-quality tequila to ensure the best possible taste. Additionally, you may want to experiment with different flavors and mix-ins, such as fruit juices, liqueurs, or even salt and lime, to create unique and enjoyable tequila-based drinks.
