## FAQ


### What is an Under Sink RO System and how does it work?

An Under Sink RO (Reverse Osmosis) System is a filtration device installed under the kitchen sink that purifies tap water by removing contaminants like chlorine, fluoride, lead, and other impurities. The system works by forcing water through a semi-permeable membrane, which traps contaminants while allowing pure water to pass through. The purified water is then stored in an adjacent storage tank and dispersed for drinking and cooking purposes. 

Under Sink RO Systems consist of a primary filter, a reverse osmosis membrane, a carbon block filter, and a storage tank. These components work together to remove impurities, provide a good taste, and ensure a reliable supply of clean water at home for daily use. They can be installed anywhere there is a sink and are generally user-friendly, requiring minimal maintenance and replacement of filters in between. 


### What benefits does an Under Sink RO System provide?

An Under Sink RO System offers several benefits, including supplying clean drinking water, removing unwanted contaminants, and improving the taste of tap water. Some of the key benefits are as follows: 
1) improved water quality due to the removal of contaminants and heavy metals, 2) a more enjoyable taste and aroma of water, 3) cost savings compared to bottled water over time, 4) the environment's benefits from reduced plastic waste from bottled water consumption, and 5) enhanced consumer protection and convenience by filtering water right at the point of use. 

Moreover, Under Sink RO Systems can be more space-saving and convenient than larger standalone or countertop systems, making them ideal for smaller kitchens and apartment dwellers with limited space. Lastly, with easy access to clean, filtered water from the tap, users can reduce their reliance on buying bottled water, reducing plastic waste and potentially saving money in the long run. 


### How often do I need to replace the filters?

The frequency of replacing the filters in an Under Sink RO System is essential for ensuring optimal water quality and system performance. Generally, the filters will last somewhere between 6 months to 2 years, depending on factors such as the hardness of the water, the system's usage, and the presence of other contaminants. 

It's a good idea to follow the specific recommendations of the filtration system manufacturer. They will provide guidelines for when the filters should be changed, which will help maintain the system's ability to provide clean, healthy water for drinking, cooking, and other household needs. Additionally, performing regular filter replacements contributes to the longevity of the equipment and helps avoid unnecessary buildup of contaminants in the system. 


### Can I install an Under Sink RO System by myself?

Under Sink RO Systems generally do not require professional installation. With proper guidance from a professional, a do-it-yourself individual can successfully install the system in their home. However, it is always recommended to consult with professional installers or read the instruction manual provided by the manufacturer for the correct placement of the filters, membranes, storage tanks, and connecting hoses. 

In case of any doubts or difficulties during the installation process, it is advisable to seek professional help. This includes determining the correct placement of the filters, membranes, storage tanks, and connecting hoses, as well as checking for any leaks before finalizing the setup. Furthermore, if you are unsure about the suitability of the system for your household needs or if you have any unique water quality concerns, consulting with a plumbing or filtration expert could be beneficial. 


### What are common issues with Under Sink RO Systems?

Some common issues users may face with Under Sink RO Systems are as follows: 
1) poor water quality from the tap, 2) inadequate water pressure, 3) leaky hoses or faucet connections, and 4) contaminants not being adequately removed. These concerns may result from incorrect installation, component malfunction, or other factors associated with the filtration system. 

To address these issues, always follow the installation and maintenance guidelines provided by the manufacturer. Also, if water quality is suboptimal, check for any potential contamination sources and if necessary, consult with a plumbing expert or water testing service to determine the appropriate solution. Proper setup and regular maintenance can help prevent many of these issues from occurring in the first place.
