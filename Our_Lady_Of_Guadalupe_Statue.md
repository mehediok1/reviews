## FAQ


### What is the significance of the Our Lady of Guadalupe statue in Catholicism?

The Our Lady of Guadalupe statue holds great significance in Catholicism, as it represents the apparition of the Virgin Mary to Saint Juan Diego, an indigenous man, in Mexico City in 1531. This event is considered a crucial moment in the history of the Catholic faith in Mexico and Latin America. The statue, often referred to as "the Guadalupana", or "Virgen de Guadalupe", is a symbol of the Virgin's love and protection, and has been a source of inspiration and devotion for centuries. It is particularly important to Mexican and Hispanic Catholics, serving as a reminder of the Guadalupan Miracle and its role in promoting religious conversion and social harmony. 

Today, the Our Lady of Guadalupe statue is displayed in churches, homes, and public spaces across the globe, as a reminder of the Virgin's unconditional love and care for all believers. It is also a popular subject in religious art and devotional practices, with many statues featuring intricate details and vibrant colors meant to evoke the warmth and grace of the Virgin Mary. 


### What materials are commonly used in making Our Lady of Guadalupe statues?

Traditionally, the Our Lady of Guadalupe statue is crafted using clay or earthen materials, as a tribute to the miraculous appearance of the Virgin on a hill named Tepeyac, overlooking Mexico City. Over the centuries, the statue has evolved to include a wider range of materials, such as wood, stone, and metal, with each material bringing its unique aesthetic and durability qualities to the design. 

To create the iconic image of Our Lady of Guadalupe, artists often use a combination of materials that evoke the ancient Aztec tradition of painting with natural pigments, combined with European techniques in wood and stone carving. Some of the most recognizable designs include a dark background, a blue cloak adorned with a gold and green band, and a bright white robe featuring intricate embroidery. Despite the use of various materials, the Our Lady of Guadalupe statue remains a testament to the cultural fusion of Aztec and European traditions, and the enduring power of faith and devotion.
