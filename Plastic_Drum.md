## Buyer's Guide

Plastic drums, also known as barrels or containers, are versatile and durable storage solutions for various applications. They come in different shapes, sizes, and materials, making it essential to know the key features to consider before purchasing one. This buyer's guide will help you understand the important aspects of plastic drums so you can make an informed decision when choosing the right product for your needs. 


### Size and Capacity

The size and capacity of a plastic drum are crucial factors to consider, as they determine the amount of material you can store. Common sizes for plastic drums range from 20 liters to 2,100 liters, with different capacity options available depending on the manufacturer. Determine the amount of liquid or solid you need to store and choose a drum with the appropriate size and capacity to avoid overfilling or underutilizing the container. 


### Material and Durability

Plastic drums are made from various materials, such as high-density polyethylene (HDPE), polyvinyl chloride (PVC), and polyethylene terephthalate (PET). Each material has its unique properties, which affect the overall strength, flexibility, and resistance to chemicals, UV rays, and temperature changes. To ensure the drum is suitable for your intended use, consider the material and its compatibility with the contents you plan to store. Check the manufacturer's specifications for information on the drum's durability and resistance to different conditions. 


### Design and Accessories

Plastic drums are available in various designs to accommodate different storage and transport needs. Some drums come with integrated handles, spigots, or stackable designs for easier handling and organization. Look for features that suit your specific handling preferences and the environment in which you plan to use the drum. Additionally, consider any accessories that may be useful, such as lids, seals, or brackets, to ensure the drum is secure and protected from external factors. 


### Certification and Safety

Ensure the plastic drum you choose meets the relevant safety and quality standards. Look for drums with certifications from recognized organizations, such as NSF International or the Food and Drug Administration (FDA). These certifications guarantee that the drum is suitable for contact with food, pharmaceuticals, or other sensitive materials. Additionally, consider the drum's safety features, such as secure lids or locking mechanisms, to protect the contents and prevent accidents during transportation or storage. 


## FAQ


### What is a plastic drum?

A plastic drum is a container used for storing or transporting various liquids and materials. These drums are typically made of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) or polyvinyl chloride (PVC), making them lightweight, durable, and resistant to chemicals and water. Plastic drums are commonly used in industries like agriculture, construction, and chemical storage, where sturdy and flexible containers with spout outlets are needed. 

While their primary function is to store liquids, some plastic drums also serve as reusable storage containers for dry goods, providing a convenient alternative to cardboard boxes or bags. With the option of selecting different sizes and shapes, plastic drums have become an indispensable tool for businesses seeking a versatile and reliable storage solution. 


### What are the advantages of plastic drums over other containers?

Compared to traditional metal or glass containers, plastic drums offer numerous advantages in terms of durability, safety, and versatility. Here are a few key benefits of using plastic drums for various storage needs: 
1) They are lightweight and easier to handle, especially when filled with liquids or heavy materials. 2) Plastic drums are resistant to corrosion, making them a better choice for storing chemicals or acids that would damage metal containers. 3) The lightweight nature of plastic drums has made shipping and transport easier and more cost-effective. 4) They are typically recyclable, which helps in reducing waste and fostering a more sustainable environment. 

Furthermore, plastic drums come in different sizes, colors, and shapes, catering to a wide range of storage needs. The most popular sizes range from 5-gallon to 55-gallon drums, but plastic drums can also be customized based on specific requirements. Thanks to their robust and reliable construction, plastic drums have become the preferred storage solution for businesses across various industries. 


### Can I reuse plastic drums after filling them?

Yes, plastic drums can be reused several times for storing and transporting different materials. Once the contents have been empty for some time, the drum should be cleaned thoroughly to remove any residues or chemicals. If the drum is to be reused for storing another product that must be stored in a clean and hygienic environment, it must pass all relevant quality inspections, including visually confirming its cleanliness, and possibly other microbiological, as well as chemical examinations. 

It is also recommended to use color-coded plastic drums, which can help identify if a drum was previously used to store a specific product or type of liquid. This practice can aid in preventing mix-ups or contaminations that may occur if the contents are not compatible. By adhering to these precautions, plastic drums can be reused effectively for a wide range of applications and industries. 


### What are the standard sizes for plastic drums?

While there is no universally accepted standard for plastic drum dimensions, the most common sizes for plastic drums are usually found in industrial applications. Here are some widely used plastic drum sizes and their approximate volumes: 5-gallon, 10-gallon, 30-gallon, 50-gallon and 55-gallon drums. For more precise measurements, it is always advisable to check the specifications provided by the manufacturer. 

It is essential to note that these dimensions can vary by manufacturer or model, and the sizes offered may differ depending on the region and intended use. However, the aforementioned sizes are generally easy to find and widely available in most markets where plastic drums are used. It is also important to consider the size of the spout opening when selecting a drum, as it may impact how well the drum can be filled or emptied. 


### What is the typical lifespan of a plastic drum?

The lifespan of a plastic drum is determined by various factors such as usage, maintenance, and material quality. With proper care and handling, a plastic drum can last for years. However, factors like exposure to harsh chemicals, temperature extremes, mechanical stresses, and UV light can impact the drum's durability. If a plastic drum is kept pristine and not exposed to such conditions, it can last for a decade or even more. 

To ensure an extended lifespan, it is crucial to follow proper storage and handling practices. For instance, drums should be stored in a dry and cool area, away from direct sunlight or harsh chemicals, and stacks should be stable to prevent damage from falling objects or excessive weight. Additionally, inspecting and replacing drums when they are damaged, leaking, or show signs of deterioration, can help extend their useful life. 


### How can I easily distinguish plastic drums that are safe for food storage?

To ensure the safety and reliability of plastic drums used for storing food products, it is vital to use containers that meet food-grade regulations. A food-grade plastic drum will be designed and manufactured to withstand the harsh environment and rigorous standards of the food industry, ensuring compliance with health and safety guidelines. 1) Check if the plastic drum is certified according to specific food-grade certifications, such as FDA (Food and Drug Administration), or EU standards. 2) Look for containers marked with a "food-grade" or "food contact" logo, which signifies their suitability for contact with edible products. 

When food-grade plastic drums are cleaned correctly and re-used, they can maintain their high standard of hygiene and quality, ensuring safety for consumers. Always follow the manufacturer's instructions for cleaning and disinfection, and dispose of any used drums responsibly in accordance with local recycling guidelines.
