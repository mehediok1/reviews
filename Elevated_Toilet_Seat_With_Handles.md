## Buyer's Guide

An elevated toilet seat with handles can be a valuable addition to any bathroom, providing comfort, convenience, and increased accessibility. This guide will help you understand the important features to consider, as well as general advice to make the best decision for your needs. 


### Features to Look For

When shopping for an elevated toilet seat with handles, pay attention to the following features: 
1. Height: The seat should be at a comfortable height for easy use. A typical height is between 17-19 inches, but there are also taller or shorter options available. 2. Material: The seat should be made of durable, easy-to-clean materials, such as plastic, bamboo, or even heated ceramic. 3. Stability: The seat should be securely attached to the toilet bowl with no wobbling or movement. Some models even come with a locking mechanism to ensure stability. 4. Handles: The handles should be comfortable to grip and made of non-slip materials to prevent accidents. Many models offer different handle designs, which can affect grip and overall stability. 


### Considerations

Before making a purchase, consider the following factors: 
1. Your specific needs: Do you need a wide seat for added comfort? Is the seat accessible for users with mobility issues? 2. The design of your bathroom: Make sure the color and style of the seat complement your bathroom's design. 3. The durability of the seat: Choose a seat made from durable materials that can withstand regular use for years. 4. The ease of installation: Some models can be installed quickly and easily without the need for plumbing expertise. 


### General Advice

1. Measure twice, buy once: Take accurate measurements of the existing toilet seat and compare them with the seat's specifications to ensure a perfect fit. 2. Check for proper installation: Make sure the elevated toilet seat is securely attached to the toilet bowl, as an improperly installed seat can cause wobbling, instability, or even damage to the toilet. 3. Clean and maintain the seat: Regularly clean the seat and handles with mild soap and water to keep it looking new and functioning properly. 4. Consider a warranty: Look for elevated toilet seats with a warranty that can provide peace of mind in case of any manufacturing defects or issues with the product. 


### Final Thoughts

An elevated toilet seat with handles can make all the difference in terms of comfort, convenience, and accessibility. By considering the important features, taking into account your specific needs, and following general advice, you can find the perfect seat for your bathroom. Remember to choose a durable, well-designed seat that meets your standards for stability and ease of use. With these tips in mind, you'll be well on your way to enjoying the benefits of an elevated toilet seat with handles. 


## FAQ


### What is the purpose of an elevated toilet seat with handles?

An elevated toilet seat with handles is designed to provide comfort and stability for individuals who have difficulty sitting or standing from a regular toilet seat, such as the elderly or people with mobility issues. The handles offer additional support and balance, making it easier to lift oneself off the seat and reducing the risk of falls. 

Moreover, the raised height of the seat provides extra space for better hygiene and cleanliness, as it prevents contact with the toilet bowl. These seats are also beneficial for people with certain medical conditions, such as hemorrhoids or arthritis, as they can help alleviate discomfort and pain during bathroom use. 


### How do I install an elevated toilet seat with handles?

Installing an elevated toilet seat with handles is a straightforward process. Most models come with detailed instructions and/or installation tools, such as a wrench or screwdriver, to assist you in the process. Generally, you will need to remove your existing toilet seat, position the new seat on the toilet, and secure it using the provided hardware. 

If you are not comfortable with the installation process, you may consider hiring a professional plumber or asking for help from a family member or friend. Many elevated toilet seats with handles are also compatible with standard toilet seats, so you can easily switch between the two depending on your needs. 


### What materials are elevated toilet seats with handles made of?

Elevated toilet seats with handles come in various materials, including plastic, wood, and metal. The choice of material will depend on your preferences, budget, and specific requirements. Plastic seats are lightweight, durable, and easy to clean, while wooden seats offer a more traditional appearance and may be more comfortable to sit on. 

Metal seats, on the other hand, may provide additional strength and sturdiness for individuals with higher body weights. Some models are also available with a combination of materials, such as a wooden seat with metal handles or a plastic seat with wooden handles, allowing you to customize the appearance and functionality of the seat. 


### Do elevated toilet seats with handles come in different sizes?

Yes, elevated toilet seats with handles are available in various sizes to accommodate different toilet shapes and sizes. You should always measure the distance between the bolts on your toilet and choose a seat that matches the measurement, ensuring a proper fit and secure installation. Some models are also adjustable, allowing you to fine-tune the height to better suit your needs. 

Additionally, it is essential to consider the weight capacity of the seat when selecting the right size. Most elevated toilet seats with handles can support up to 300-400 pounds, but it is always best to check the manufacturer's specifications to ensure the seat can safely accommodate your weight and usage. 


### How do I clean an elevated toilet seat with handles?

Cleaning an elevated toilet seat with handles is relatively simple. Most seats are made from materials that are easy to clean, such as plastic or wood. You can use a standard household cleaner, warm water, and a soft cloth to gently wipe down the seat, handles, and any other surfaces. For a more thorough cleaning, you can disassemble the seat, if possible, and soak each part in a cleaning solution before rinsing and drying thoroughly. 

If you have any specific cleaning requirements, such as using antimicrobial or hypoallergenic products, you should consult the manufacturer's instructions or guidelines. It is essential to maintain proper hygiene when using an elevated toilet seat with handles to ensure your comfort and safety during bathroom use. 


### How do I store an elevated toilet seat with handles when not in use?

Storing an elevated toilet seat with handles when not in use depends on the design and size of the seat. Some models have a convenient storage feature, such as a hinged lid or built-in locking mechanism, that allows you to close the seat and secure it in place. This can prevent the seat from accidentally falling down or being tampered with by others. 

If your seat does not have a built-in storage feature, you can consider storing it on a nearby shelf or hanging it on a wall using a specialized toilet seat hanger. You should always ensure that the seat is securely stored when not in use to prevent injury or damage. Additionally, you may want to store the seat in a protective cover or bag to shield it from dust, moisture, or other elements that could affect its longevity and quality.
