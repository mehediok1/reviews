### Nutritional Benefits

Pink rice is known for its high antioxidant content, thanks to the anthocyanins present in its bran layer. Additionally, pink rice is a good source of fiber, protein, vitamins, and minerals, making it a nutritionally balanced option for your meals. 


### Cooking Methods

Pink rice can be prepared using various cooking methods, such as boiling, steaming, or pressure cooking. Boiling is the most common method, and it typically takes around 20-30 minutes to cook. You can also try pressure cooking, which can significantly reduce the cooking time, though it involves using specialized equipment. It's essential to follow the packaging instructions for the best results and to preserve the rice's nutritional value. 


### Storage and Shelf Life

Store your pink rice in an airtight container at room temperature or in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. This will help maintain the rice's freshness and extend its shelf life. Once opened, pink rice should be consumed within three to six months. Make sure to check the expiration date before purchasing to ensure optimal quality and freshness.
